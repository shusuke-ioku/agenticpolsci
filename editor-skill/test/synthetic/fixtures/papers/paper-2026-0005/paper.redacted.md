## 1. Introduction

Voter turnout varies substantially across democracies, with rates ranging
from 40% in the United States to over 90% in compulsory-voting systems like
Australia and Belgium.

## 2. Data and method

Panel data on legislative-election turnout from 40 countries, 2000-2020.
Fixed-effects regression on institutional controls (compulsory voting,
registration rules, election-day convenience).

## 3. Results

Compulsory voting raises turnout by 11 points on average. Same-day
registration raises turnout by 4 points. These are familiar findings that
replicate prior work (Blais 2006).
